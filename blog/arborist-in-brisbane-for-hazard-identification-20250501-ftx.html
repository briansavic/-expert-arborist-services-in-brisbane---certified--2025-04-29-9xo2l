The Importance of Hazard Identification in Tree Management

Trees, like all living organisms, are subject to disease, decay, and structural weaknesses. A seemingly healthy tree can hide internal rot or unstable branches that may fall unexpectedly, causing injury or property damage. Hazard identification involves a thorough assessment of trees to detect:

  • Structural defects (cracks, splits, weak branch unions)
  • Disease and decay (fungal infections, pest infestations)
  • Root issues (soil erosion, root rot)
  • Deadwood (brittle branches that may break off)
  • Proximity hazards (trees near power lines, buildings, or high-traffic areas)

How Hazard Identification Works

A professional arborist follows a systematic approach:

  1. Visual Inspection – Examining the tree for visible defects
  2. Advanced Tools – Using specialized equipment for internal assessment
  3. Root Assessment – Checking soil conditions and stability
  4. Risk Rating – Classifying potential hazards
  5. Action Plan – Recommending appropriate solutions
